Discovery DSC: Preparing Standard Aluminum Hermetic Pans

The standard aluminum hermetic pans require the use of the white die set shown in the figure below.

 

There is one lower white die (larger diameter) and two upper white dies. Both upper dies must be used to seal the standard aluminum hermetic pans. Each die has been inscribed with a number indicating the order in which they will be used during the sealing process.

Follow these basic instructions to use the Tzero sample press and the Standard Aluminum Hermetic pans:

  1. Select the desired sample pan and lid and obtain the matching die set. The die sets have colored rings and the pan/lid boxes have matching colored labels.
  2. NOTE: Always handle the pans and lids carefully with tweezers. Ensure that any and all surfaces that the pans are placed on are clean and free of any possible contaminants.
  3. Weigh the empty pan and lid.
  4. NOTE: Only the Discovery DSC, Q2000 and Q1000 use the weight of the pan and lid (in Advanced Tzero mode) to improve the resolution and sensitivity of your heat flow measurement. If you carefully measure and enter the empty sample and reference pan weights into the software, the effect of the difference between the sample and reference pan weights will be compensated for and will improve your heat flow measurement. It is highly recommended that you take the extra time to do so. Users of the other Q Series and earlier DSC instruments do not need to perform this step.
  5. Prepare the sample and carefully place the sample in the pan. If you are using a powder or granular sample, spread it evenly in the pan. Do not allow the sample to spill onto the lip of the pan.
  6. Weigh the filled sample pan and lid and determine the sample weight. It is important to control the amount of sample you are using. Refer to additional material in the training courses for optimal sample size considerations.
  7. Place the filled sample pan in the lower die and position the matching lid in place, concave side up.
  8. Push the press handle back and place the upper white die #1 into the press. The upper die slides in easily and a magnet will hold it in place.

  1. Place the lower die with the prepared sample pan and lid into the press. Rotate the die slightly from to ensure that it is seated properly.
  2. Pull the handle forward until it stops to form the edges of the sealing surface prior to finishing the seal.
  3. Push the press handle back and remove the lower die and pan and then the upper die #1.
  4. Install the upper white die #2 into the press.

  1. Replace the lower white die with the "formed" pan into the press. Rotate the die to ensure that it is seated properly.
  2. Pull the handle forward until it stops. This step requires more force than the other die and pan types, this is normal.
  3. CAUTION: Make sure that you hold the handle firmly throughout the entire crimping procedure!
  4. Push the handle back again and remove the lower die and pan.
  5. Use the pan removal tool on the front of the press to remove the sample pan. The sample is ready to be tested on the instrument.
  6. Prepare an empty pan and lid for use as the reference pan. It is important that the same care be taken in preparing the reference pan as in preparing the sample pan.
