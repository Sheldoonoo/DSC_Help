Discovery DSC: Using the Tzero® Press

The following basic instructions can be applied to any set of dies used with the Tzero press. For more details on preparing specific types of sample pans, see the following topics.

  • Preparing Tzero Aluminum Pans and Tzero Low-Mass Aluminum Pans (Black die set)
  • Preparing Tzero Aluminum Hermetic Pans (Blue die set)
  • Preparing Standard Aluminum Pans (Green die set)
  • Preparing Standard Aluminum Hermetic Pans (White die set)
  • Preparing High Volume Pans (Yellow die set)

Follow these basic instructions to use the Tzero sample press:

  1. Select the desired sample pan and matching lid and obtain the matching die set. The die sets have colored rings and the pan/lid boxes have matching colored labels.
  2. NOTE: Always handle the pans and lids carefully with tweezers. Ensure that all surfaces that the pans are placed on are clean and free of any possible contaminants.
  3. Weigh the empty pan and lid.
  4. NOTE: Only the Discovery DSC, Q2000, and Q1000 use the weight of the pan and lid (in Advanced Tzero mode T4P) to improve the resolution and sensitivity of your heat flow measurement. If you carefully measure and enter the empty sample and reference pan weights into the software, the effect of the difference between the sample and reference pan weights will be compensated for and will improve your heat flow measurement. It is highly recommended that you take the extra time to do so. Users of the other Q Series and earlier DSC instruments do not need to perform this step.
  5. Prepare the sample and place it in the pan. If you are using a powder or granular sample, spread it evenly in the pan. Make sure no sample material is on the lip of the pan.
  6. Weigh the filled sample pan and lid and determine the sample weight. It is important to control the amount of sample you are using. Refer to additional material in the training courses for optimal sample size considerations. Refer to DSC Sample Preparation for additional information.
  7. Place the filled sample pan in the lower die and position the matching lid in place.
  8. Move the press handle back and place the matching upper die into the press. The upper die slides in easily and a magnet will hold it in place.
  9. Place the lower die with the prepared sample pan into the press. Rotate the die slightly to ensure that it is seated properly.
  10. Pull the handle forward until it stops to seal the lid to the pan.
  11. CAUTION: Make sure that you hold the handle firmly throughout the entire crimping procedure!

  1. Move the handle back again and remove the lower die and pan.

For Standard Aluminum pans, turn the lower die set upside down.The sample is ready to be tested on the instrument.
