  • CAUTION: The improper use of the cell conditioning tool could cause irreparable damage to the DSC cell surface; use of this tool is NOT covered under warranty. The pure silver cell block is extremely malleable and can be scratched, gouged, or dented if care is not taken. If you have questions or concerns before using this tool, please contact your local service representative or TA Instruments.
  • WARNING: Ensure that the cell is at room temperature before starting the procedure described below.

Parts required:

  • 972581.901: Cell conditioning tool kit
  • 100092.911: Disks replacement 1000 grit
  • 202333.001: Goo Gone 1 oz.

For optimum performance, the conditioning tool should be used to improve the contact between the inner lid and the cell. Use of this tool can improve all aspects of baseline performance: start up, linearity, repeatability, and slope.

The cell surface should be lightly conditioned on a regular basis to remove tarnish that develops over time and usage. Frequency depends on the time and usage between conditionings, typical intervals are every few months of regular usage.

  • WARNING: Only remove the thin surface layer when conditioning.

In particular, condition the cell surface whenever:

  • You notice changes in the baseline startup region (indicative of a potential lid contact issue)
  • If you have just changed the lids
  • Before you do a Tzero calibration

Conditioning the Cell

To condition the cell, follow the instructions below:

  1. CAUTION: To clean the coated lid, only use solvents such as IPA, Methanol, or Acetone, and lint free cloth or wipes. Never use sand paper on the coated lid as the thin layer of coating will be removed and it will not perform as intended by the design.
  2. Before using the conditioning tool, lightly clean the cell surface with a lint-free wipe so that the tool cannot ground any debris or dust into the silver surface.
  3. Note the condition of the silver surface before placing the tool on the cell. The purpose of using this tool is to remove any oxide layer and /or visible scratches from the silver and leave the surface as smooth as possible.
  4. Gently place the tool on the cell without applying pressure. Slowly turn the tool clockwise one-quarter of a turn. Lift the tool straight up out of the cell and wipe the silver surface with a lint free wipe or swab. If using a swab, make sure to blow out any loose fibers or particles that may shed during the cleaning process. Refer to the figures below.

  1. Repeat this process until the surface is smooth and shiny with no visible scratches.

Changing the Sanding Discs

To change the sanding discs, carefully peel off the used disk and remove the residual adhesive with Goo Gone (or equivalent) and a lint free wipe.
