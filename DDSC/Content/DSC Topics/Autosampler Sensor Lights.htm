Understanding the DSC Autosampler Sensor Indicator Lights

At the bottom of all the Autosampler calibration user interface screens are ten red and green sensor indicator lights. See the following table for an explanation of these sensors.

 

 

Sensor Light Functions
Sensor Lights Description
Tray Present
Indicates whether or not an Autosampler tray is present. RED equals no tray present and GREEN means a tray is present or on.
Tilt
Indicates the arm tilt switch condition. RED means the arm is in the normal position and GREEN means that the arm is tilted up.
Pan Optic Indicates the fiber optic amplifier output condition, which senses the presence of a pan. RED means no pan present and GREEN means a pan is present.
Pan Elec Indicates the electric pan sensor condition, which senses the presence of a pan. RED means no pan present and GREEN means a pan is present.
Moving Indicates whether or not the Autosampler arm is currently in motion. RED indicates autosampler is stationary and GREEN indicates autosampler is in motion.
Tray Home Indicates whether or not the Autosampler tray is in the home position. GREEN equals home position and RED means it is not in the home position.
Up Home Indicates whether or not the Autosampler arm is in the up home position. GREEN equals home position and RED means it is not in the home position.
Rotate Home Indicates whether or not the Autosampler arm is in the rotate home position. GREEN equals home position and RED means it is not in the home position.
Extend Home Indicates whether or not the Autosampler arm is in the up home position. GREEN equals home position and RED means it is not in the home position.
Grip Home Indicates whether or not the Autosampler arm is in the grip home position. GREEN equals home position and RED means it is not in the home position.
