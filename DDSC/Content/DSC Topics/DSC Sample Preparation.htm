Determining Sample Size

Normally, sample weight in DSC experiments is in the range of 5 to 20 milligrams. For better sensitivity in detecting a weak transition, choose a large sample size. For better resolution to separate overlapping transitions, choose a small sample size. If purity determinations are to be performed, then sample sizes of 1 to 3 mg are recommended. Refer to the table below to guide you when selecting the sample size and heating rates for your experiment.

Determining Sample Size
Types of Measurement Sample Size Heating Rate (°C/min)

Melting point 2 to 10 5 to 10
Kinetics
(Borchardt and Daniels) 		5 to 10 5 to 20

Purity 1 to 3 0.5 to 1
Crystallinity 5 to 10 5 to 10

MDSC 2 to 10 1 to 5

* Use larger sample sizes at slower heating rates, and smaller sample sizes at higher rates.
