After exposure, the material is cooled and then heated at a constant rate (e.g., 10°C/minute). The heat associated with the reaction exotherm, measured as the material is reheated, should be half that found on heating the initial unexposed material. If it is, the model is verified.

Experimental

In a preliminary DSC experiment, the substance of interest is measured at relatively high heating rates, e.g. 10 or 20°C/min in order to quickly estimate the initial and final temperatures as well as the heating rates for the subsequent measurements. Once the transition range is determined, three or more samples are prepared and analyzed at different heating rates (e.g. 2, 5, 10 and 20°C/min.) In the DSC experiment, the peaks are integrated across the entire conversion range. Once all of the experiments are integrated, the software can perform the relevant calculations.
