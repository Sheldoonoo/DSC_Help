Overview

The isothermal kinetics approach can be applied theoretically to both nth order and autocatalyzed exothermic systems. (Autocatalyzed systems are characterized by the formation of some intermediate species which markedly accelerates the reaction. Epoxy systems used in industry, for example are autocatalyzed systems). It should be noted, however, that this approach is generally not used to model either endothermic reactions or crystallization kinetics.

Experimental

The experimental details to perform an isothermal kinetics analysis are very straightforward. To select the proper temperatures for the required isothermal experiments, the sample is first analyzed at 5°C/minute, usually resulting in a broad exothermic peak. An isothermal temperature is chosen that lies between a temperature 10–20°C below the onset of reaction and a temperature midway to the peak maximum. When performing the actual isothermal experiments, the DSC cell is heated to the desired isothermal temperature using an “initial temperature” program segment. The cell is then rapidly opened, the sample loaded, and the cell re-closes. When the displayed temperature is within 4°C of the desired isothermal temperature, data collection is initiated. Isothermal kinetics can also be applied to reactions which are initiated through the introduction of UV light, as is typical when using the Photocalorimeter Accessory (PCA).

Additional Plots

Two predictive curves are obtained from the Sestak Berggren Isothermal Kinetics modeling: isothermal plots and isoconversion plots. Isothermal plots provide time conditions and degree of conversion information for specific isothermal temperatures. Isoconversion plots provide time and temperature conditions for a specific conversion level. Both predictive plots are useful in the development and control of reaction conditions in order to achieve the desired final product.

  1. Display conversion plot.
  2. From the File Manager, select the conversion plot and then right-click and select Transformations... > Kinetics Conversion...

  1. A Kinetics Conversion dialog to define the desired conversion plot as well as the parameters of that plot displays.

  1. A new document is created for this plot.

  • Half Life (vs. Temperature)
  • Conversion Time (vs. Temperature)
  • Percent Conversion (vs. Time)

Many users prefer to use Avrami Kinetics applied to autocatalytic reactions such as thermoset cure and crystallization.

This equation is coupled with the logarithmic form of the Arrhenius equation to provide the effect of temperature.

Where:

Z = the pre-exponential factor (1/sec)

Eα = activation energy (J/mol)

R = gas constant (8.314 J/(mol K))

T = absolute temperature (K)

From data from a single isothermal test (gather in the same manner as other isothermal tests), the fraction-remaining-to-react (α), and elapsed time (t) at a constant temperature (T) are recorded. A plot of ln[-ln(1-α)] versus ln[t] is prepared for a series of ≈20 points across the reaction exotherm. A best fit straight line is fit to the data. The slope of the line equals reaction order p and the intercept is ln[k].

In a similar manner, values for ln[k] are obtained over a range of temperatures (≈10 K). A second plot is prepared of ln[k] versus 1/T for this series of experiments and the data fit to a straight line. The slope equals –Eα /R (from which may be calculated) and the intercept is ln[Z].
